THREAT LIBRARY

Know the Top Threats

Understand how each scam works and learn specific prevention tips to keep yourself and your family safe.

Email & SMS

Phishing Attacks

Fraudulent emails, SMS, or websites that impersonate trusted entities (banks, govt portals) to steal your login credentials, OTPs, or financial data.

Prevention Tips
  • Never click links in unsolicited SMS/emails
  • Verify sender domain — banks never ask for OTP
  • Enable 2FA on all important accounts
Financial

UPI / Payment Fraud

Scammers trick victims into approving UPI collect requests, scanning malicious QR codes, or sharing screen access via apps like AnyDesk to drain bank accounts.

Prevention Tips
  • You never need to pay to receive money
  • Never scan unknown QR codes for “refunds”
  • Decline all unexpected UPI collect requests
Identity

Social Media Hacking

Attackers compromise Instagram, Facebook, or WhatsApp accounts through phishing links, SIM swapping, or weak passwords, then impersonate victims to extort contacts.

Prevention Tips
  • Use unique, strong passwords per platform
  • Enable login alerts & two-factor auth
  • Never share OTPs with anyone, even “friends”
Employment

Job & Task Scams

Fake job offers on Telegram or WhatsApp promise easy work-from-home income. Victims are asked to complete tasks, invest money upfront, and are ghosted once payments increase.

Prevention Tips
  • Legitimate jobs never require deposits
  • Verify company through official website & LinkedIn
  • “Earn 5K/day from home” is always a scam
Financial

Investment Fraud

Ponzi schemes, fake crypto trading platforms, and stock market WhatsApp groups lure victims with guaranteed returns. Initial small payouts build trust before large sums vanish.

Prevention Tips
  • “Guaranteed returns” = guaranteed scam
  • Only invest through SEBI-registered platforms
  • Verify with RBI/SEBI before transferring funds
Technical

Malware & Ransomware

Malicious software delivered through email attachments, fake apps, or infected websites that encrypts your files or steals data, demanding cryptocurrency ransom for recovery.

Prevention Tips
  • Install apps only from official stores
  • Keep OS and software always updated
  • Maintain offline backups of critical data
SAFETY PROTOCOL

The Cyber Safety Checklist

A quick-reference guide with essential do's and don'ts for everyday digital safety.

Do's — Best Practices

Use strong, unique passwords (12+ chars, mixed) for every account
Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) on email, banking & social media
Verify URLs before entering credentials — look for HTTPS and correct domain
Keep operating systems, browsers, and apps updated regularly
Review bank statements & UPI transaction history weekly
Report suspicious activity immediately — Call 1930
Use a reputable password manager instead of reusing passwords

Don'ts — Common Mistakes

Never share OTP, CVV, PIN, or passwords with anyone — not even “bank staff”
Don’t install apps via links sent on WhatsApp or Telegram
Don’t click “Verify Now” or “Account Suspended” links from SMS/email
Don’t install remote-access apps (AnyDesk, TeamViewer) on request
Don’t send money to “receive” a prize, refund, or lottery winnings
Don’t use public Wi-Fi for banking or entering sensitive credentials
Don’t overshare personal info (DOB, address) on social media profiles
LEGAL FRAMEWORK

Know Your Rights

Key sections of the Information Technology Act, 2000 that protect you against cybercrimes in India.

Section 66

Computer-Related Offences

Covers hacking, data theft, and any dishonest or fraudulent act performed using a computer resource — including unauthorized access and data manipulation.

Up to 3 years imprisonment + fine
Section 66C

Identity Theft

Penalizes anyone who fraudulently uses another person’s electronic signature, password, or unique identification feature for impersonation or unauthorized transactions.

Up to 3 years imprisonment + up to Rs. 1 lakh fine
Section 66D

Cheating by Personation

Applies to impersonation using any communication device or computer resource — covers fake customer care numbers, fraudulent websites, and phishing scams.

Up to 3 years imprisonment + up to Rs. 1 lakh fine
Section 66E

Violation of Privacy

Protects against capturing, publishing, or transmitting a person’s private images without consent — directly addresses sextortion and non-consensual image sharing.

Up to 3 years imprisonment + up to Rs. 2 lakh fine
Section 43

Unauthorized Access & Damage

Provides civil liability for unauthorized access, downloading data, introducing viruses, or disrupting computer systems. Victims can claim compensation for damages suffered.

Compensation up to Rs. 5 crore per incident
Section 67

Obscene Content Online

Criminalizes publishing or transmitting obscene material in electronic form. Extended by 67A (sexually explicit content) and 67B (child sexual abuse material).

Up to 5 years imprisonment + up to Rs. 10 lakh fine
