FIRST 60 MINUTES

Immediate Actions

Time is critical. Follow these steps in order during the first hour after discovering a cyber attack or financial fraud.

Minute 0–5

Call 1930 Immediately

Dial the National Cyber Crime Helpline. Report the incident with whatever details you have — transaction IDs, phone numbers, amounts. The operator will initiate a hold on the fraudulent transaction chain.

Minute 5–10

Contact Your Bank

Call your bank's fraud helpline (numbers below) and request an immediate freeze on your account. Ask for a complaint reference number. If UPI fraud, also contact NPCI.

Minute 10–20

Secure All Accounts

Change passwords on banking apps, email, and social media. Enable two-factor authentication everywhere. Log out of all active sessions from unknown devices.

Minute 20–30

Preserve All Evidence

Take screenshots of transactions, messages, call logs, and any suspicious emails. Do NOT delete anything. Save SMS alerts, bank statements, and chat histories.

Minute 30–45

File Online Complaint

Visit cybercrime.gov.in and file a formal complaint. Upload your screenshots and evidence. Note down the complaint acknowledgment number for tracking.

Minute 45–60

File a Police FIR

Visit your nearest police station or cyber cell. Carry printed copies of all evidence, your complaint acknowledgment from 1930, and bank statements. Insist on getting a written FIR copy.
